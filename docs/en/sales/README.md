# Overview of new features

This section contains descriptions of new features and **functionalities of WebJET CMS in plain language**, without unnecessary technical formulations. It is not technical documentation. The goal is **to clearly explain what individual features bring to customers and why they are valuable to them**.

## Who is this section for?

This section is intended for anyone who needs to quickly understand the benefits of the WebJET CMS solution:

- **traders**
- **pre-sales consultants**
- **project managers**
- **marketing teams**
- **customers and client-side decision-makers**

The information is formulated in a way that it can be **directly used in customer communication** — in offers, presentations, meetings, or in answers to questions like "why WebJET?".

## Customer groups

We distinguish three main customer groups:

- **Government** — public sector organizations, ministries, offices, local governments. They emphasize security, legislative compliance, accessibility (WCAG), and long-term support.
- **Commercial sector** — businesses, corporations, e-commerce. They are interested in speed of deployment, flexibility, third-party integrations, and return on investment.
- **Programmers / Technical Partners** — internal developers of the customer or supplier companies. They are interested in API, extensibility, code quality, documentation, and the possibility of custom modifications.

> **Important:** The boundaries between groups are blurring. Today, both government and commercial sectors demand the ability to customize their products (even if they often don't actually do it themselves, they want to have the option). Therefore, information about technical extensibility is relevant to everyone.

## Documentation structure

The feature descriptions are organized by year — each year has its own file:

- [Year 2026](2026/README.md) — features delivered in 2026
- [Year 2025](2025/README.md) — features delivered in 2025
- [Year 2024](2024/README.md) — features delivered in 2024

New records are added to the top of the respective annual file (newest at the top). Each record contains:

1. A clear name for the feature (not technical)
2. Explanation of what it is and what the customer gets from it
3. List of specific benefits
4. Images or links to documentation
